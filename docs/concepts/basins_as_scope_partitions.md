# Basins as Partitions of a Higher-Level Scope

## Overview

Within the ARW interpretation, basins are not merely dynamical artifacts.
They can be understood as **partitions of a higher-level scope** induced by a reduction
in admissible observables.

---

## Observable Reduction

Let the higher-level scope contain a set of observables:

Π_H

A reduced scope selects a subset:

Π_L ⊂ Π_H

This reduction suppresses some degrees of freedom while keeping others.

---

## Emergent Partition

Under reduced observables, many microstates become indistinguishable.

This induces a partition of the higher-level state space:

X = B₁ ∪ B₂ ∪ ... ∪ Bₙ

These regions correspond to **basins**.

Each basin represents a region that remains structurally stable under the reduced
observational regime.

---

## Basin Stability

A basin remains meaningful as long as enough observables remain admissible to
interpret its structure.

If too many observables lose admissibility, basins may:

- merge
- split
- or disappear

leading to a scope transition.

---

## Interpretation

Basins therefore represent **stable regions of a higher-level state space under
observable reduction**.

They are emergent structures arising from the relationship between observables,
boundary conditions, and admissibility.
