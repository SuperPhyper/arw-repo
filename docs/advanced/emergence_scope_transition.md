# Emergence as Scope Transition

## Overview

Within the ARW framework, emergence is interpreted as a **transition between descriptive scopes**.
A scope is a stabilized descriptive regime defined by a set of observables that remain
admissible under the system's boundary conditions.

Emergence occurs when some observables **lose admissibility**, forcing a reorganization of
the effective state space and the stabilization of a new scope.

---

## Core Idea

A scope remains valid as long as its observables remain admissible.

When certain observables become dynamically incompatible with the system's boundary
conditions, they lose their stabilizing role.

This leads to:

1. Loss of admissibility of some observables
2. Reorganization of the effective state space
3. Stabilization of a new scope

Thus emergence is not the creation of new laws, but the **transition between regimes of
description**.

---

## Structural Sequence

Emergence can be understood as the following sequence:

Old scope (S₁)
→ loss of admissibility of some observables
→ reorganization of state space
→ stabilization of new scope (S₂)

This transition is often continuous in the system's dynamics even though the
descriptive regimes themselves are discrete.

---

## Interpretation

Emergence therefore reflects a structural adaptation of description when the
previous descriptive regime no longer provides stable ordering.
