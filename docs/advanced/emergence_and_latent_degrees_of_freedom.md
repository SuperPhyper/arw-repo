# Emergence and Latent Degrees of Freedom

## Overview

A central mechanism behind emergence in ARW is the interaction between
**latent degrees of freedom** and admissible observables.

Latent degrees of freedom are variables that remain dynamically active even when
they are suppressed by the current descriptive scope.

---

## Latent Dynamics

When a scope is defined, some variables are treated as stabilized or negligible.
However, these variables may remain dynamically active.

Over time they can:

- accumulate correlations
- influence system behavior
- destabilize the current scope

---

## Activation of Latent Variables

Emergence occurs when previously suppressed degrees of freedom become relevant.

This can happen when:

- boundary conditions change
- coupling strengths increase
- long-term correlations accumulate

Once this happens, the current scope may lose admissibility.

---

## Resulting Dynamics

The activation of latent degrees of freedom can lead to:

- instability
- reorganization of the state space
- formation of new basins
- stabilization of a new scope

---

## Interpretation

From the ARW perspective, emergence is therefore driven by the activation of
latent degrees of freedom that were previously suppressed by the current scope.

This mechanism connects:

- admissibility
- scope dominance
- basin formation
- and scope transition.
