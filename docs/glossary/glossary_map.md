# Glossary Map

This map provides a structural overview of how the main ARW/ART concepts relate.

```text
Boundary Conditions
        ↓
Resonance / Coupling
        ↓
Latent Degrees of Freedom
        ↓
Admissibility
        ↓
Scope Dominance
        ↓
Scope Transition
        ↓
Emergence
        ↓
Basin / Partition
        ↓
Emergent Solution Space
```

## Interpretation

- Boundary conditions act as structural filters.
- Coupling and resonance determine which interactions accumulate.
- Latent degrees of freedom may destabilize a scope.
- Admissibility measures whether the scope remains valid.
- Scope dominance determines whether the current description still orders the system.
- Scope transition marks the shift to a new descriptive regime.
- Emergence describes the stabilization of new structure.
- Basins and partitions are the higher-level regions that become visible.
- Emergent solution spaces organize the viable trajectories or behaviors at the new scope.
